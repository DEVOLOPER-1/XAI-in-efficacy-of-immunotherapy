🔍 THREE XAI TECHNIQUES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
   Modality: TABULAR (genomic + clinical features)
   Level: Global (all patients) + Local (per-patient)
   Output: Feature importance bars + waterfall plots
   Use Case: Identify top drivers (e.g., TP53 mutation + CD8A expression)
   Figure: 2a - SHAP summary bar chart (top 15 features)

2. LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
   Modality: TABULAR (local explanations)
   Level: Local (single patient)
   Output: Linear regression coefficients on perturbed data
   Use Case: Explain why patient P42 has high TMB prediction
   Figure: 2b - LIME waterfall plot (patient-specific)

3. GRAD-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping)
   Modality: IMAGE (WSI patches)
   Level: Spatial (per-pixel attention)
   Output: Heatmaps (red=attended, blue=ignored)
   Use Case: Visualize tissue morphology patterns driving prediction
   Figures: 3a–b - Grad-CAM overlays (high-TMB vs. low-TMB patients)

BONUS: CellMorphNet Cellular Deconvolution
   Modality: IMAGE (cell-type interpretability)
   Output: Per-patient cell-type percentages (T-cells, macrophages, …)
   Use Case: Compare predicted T-cell % to IHC ground truth
   Figure: 3c - Cell-type stacked bar chart (high vs. low TMB cohorts)

KEY INSIGHT: XAI enables bias detection, builds clinician trust, validates models

⚖️ ETHICAL GUARDRAILS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ALGORITHMIC BIAS MITIGATION
   ├─ Audit: Stratify val set by age, sex, race, stage
   ├─ Test: Welch's t-test (p < 0.05 flags disparity)
   ├─ Analyze: SHAP importance varies by demographic?
   └─ Mitigate: Balanced resampling or fairness loss if bias found

2. DATA PRIVACY & COMPLIANCE
   ├─ HIPAA: Audit trails (git SHA + config fingerprint)
   ├─ GDPR: Right to erasure (can delete patient data, keep model)
   ├─ Retention: Raw WSI → 7 days; features → 12 months; explanations → 30 days (auto-purge)
   ├─ Encryption: AES-256 at-rest, TLS 1.3 in-transit
   └─ Access Control: Role-based (pathology team only for WSI)

3. CLINICAL OVERRELIANCE PREVENTION
   ├─ Confidence Intervals: Not just ŷ, but [CI_lower, CI_upper]
   ├─ Explanation Cards: SHAP + Grad-CAM per patient (clinician validates)
   ├─ Risk Stratification: Low/Medium/High categories (actionable)
   ├─ Human-in-the-loop: Physician must review before treatment decision
   └─ Model Card: Document assumptions, limitations, demographics
